## Simulation Plots and Thermal-Fluidic Analysis

The following simulation plots present thermo-fluidic analyses performed for the optimization of microchannel heat sink geometries intended for chip-level electronic cooling applications.

To enhance heat-transfer performance, maintain thermal uniformity in hotspot regions, and reduce the overall pressure drop for improved pumping efficiency, four novel microchannel geometries were developed and analyzed:

- **Radial Arc Microchannel (RAMC)**
- **Multiple Pin Microchannel (MPMC)**
- **Modified Radial Microchannel (MRMC)**
- **Spiral Microchannel (SMC)**

The performance of these geometries was evaluated in comparison with the conventionally employed **Radial Microchannel (RMC)** heat sink structure.

---

### Plot Descriptions

#### Temperature Distribution at Different Flow Rates

The plots on the left illustrate temperature profiles obtained across six different inlet flow rates, demonstrating the influence of flow conditions on hotspot cooling and thermal uniformity.

#### Temperature Distribution under Varying Hotspot Heat Flux

The central plots present temperature distributions corresponding to different inward hotspot heat flux conditions, enabling evaluation of thermal response and heat dissipation capability under varying thermal loads.

#### Pressure and Velocity Profiles

The plots on the right display:
- pressure distribution profiles,
- and velocity-field characteristics

obtained at fixed heat-flux and inlet-velocity conditions for comparative thermo-fluidic analysis.

### Computational Framework

The simulations were performed using:

- **COMSOL Multiphysics**
- Finite Element Method (FEM)
- Coupled transport equations
- Heat-transfer modeling
- Fluid-flow simulations

---

### Objectives of the Study

- Improve hotspot thermal management
- Enhance heat-transfer efficiency
- Minimize overall pressure drop
- Improve pumping efficiency
- Investigate thermo-fluidic behavior across novel microchannel geometries
